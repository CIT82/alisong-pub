About our Founder

His Impact on the San Francisco Community

Our Founder's Impact

Hiroshi Tatsuya has been a generous member of the community since day one of opening Soy Full. He takes pride in his Bay Area community and believes in giving back to the community that has supported him for so many years.

  • Hiroshi's Impact

  • Helping San Francisco's Unhoused

    Hiroshi regularly donates his time and money to various homeless shelters across the city. He prepares large batches of ramen for the shelter patrons so they can have a hot meal three days a week.

  • Donates to Schools

    Hiroshi makes frequent monetary donations to low income schools in the Bay Area that lack funding for the arts programs. He also donates boxes of supplies at the beginning of each school year.

  • Teaches Cooking Classes

    Hiroshi teaches two free weekly cooking classes at the city Recreational center, one for children and one for seniors. He wants to inspire a passion for cooking in the current generation and the next generation!

  • Public Garden

    Hiroshi uses his own personal garden in the heart of San Francisco as a community food garden, where the produce is open to anyone who needs it. He teaches the children of the neighborhood how to grow healthy food.


  • How This Adds Up

  • Feeding the Unhoused

    Hiroshi's generosity provides hundreds of free, healthy, hearty meals to hte unhoused population of San Francisco each and every week. The patrons of the local shelters love to see him walk through the door.

  • School Donations

    After Hiroshi's generous donations, the local schools of the Bay Area saw a sharp increase in arts program enrollment. His donations provided thousands of children with much needed art supplies!

  • Feeding the World

    The community garden Hiroshi manages feeds over a hundred children facing food insecurity while simultaneously teaching them about the benefits of healthy home grown vegetables.
